ABOUT

Rhea Dudani is a filmmaker, driven by her passion for feminism and politics, specializing in crafting narratives that amplify South Asian and female voices to shatter barriers.

Her filmmaking journey began uniquely, with her debut in 2008—a whimsical production shot on her mother's camcorder, starring her sister and an eclectic cast of household plants.

With a B.F.A. in Film and Television from the Tisch School of the Arts, Rhea now works as a freelance producer, director, and writer.

Previously, Rhea thrived as a freelance Associate Producer at STUDIO ONE, contributing to a forthcoming feature documentary set for release in late 2024. Her career boasts diverse experiences, including pivotal roles as an Associate Producer at AKPD Message & Media, where she shaped poignant political ads for the 2022 Midterm Election.

Embracing film as a catalyst for societal transformation, Rhea continues to pioneer unconventional storytelling, igniting conversations and challenging perceptions with each cinematic endeavor.
